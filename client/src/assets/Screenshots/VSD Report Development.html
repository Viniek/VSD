VSD is one of the most common congenital heart defects in infants and can range from small, self-resolving holes to large defects that require surgical intervention. Early identification of VSD is critical to prevent complications such as heart failure, pulmonary hypertension, and arrhythmias. Conventional methods for diagnosing VSD include echocardiography, electrocardiograms (ECGs), and chest X-rays.
